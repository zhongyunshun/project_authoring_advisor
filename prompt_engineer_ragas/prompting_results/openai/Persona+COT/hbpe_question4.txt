The roles and responsibilities concerning the maintenance and administration of Humber Bay Park East, as detailed in the "HBPE Project Brief - Phase I," include collaboration between the Toronto Region Conservation Authority (TRCA) and the City of Toronto. 

1. **TRCA Responsibilities**:
   - The TRCA is primarily responsible for the overall project management, including the planning, design, and implementation of maintenance activities.
   - It oversees environmental assessments and ensures adherence to conservation principles in any maintenance or development work at the site.
   - The TRCA conducts studies and evaluations of site conditions and implements necessary shoreline protection measures to maintain park integrity and safety.

2. **City of Toronto Responsibilities**:
   - The City of Toronto works in conjunction with TRCA on various project aspects, including local policy alignment and public engagement.
   - The city is responsible for maintaining the broader infrastructure in the park, ensuring that any municipal standards are met.
   - It collaborates with TRCA to facilitate communication with the community and provide feedback during the project phases.

The collaborative effort emphasizes a shared commitment to enhancing and preserving Humber Bay Park East while addressing ecological and recreational needs for the public. 

For further details, you can refer to specific sections of the "HBPE Project Brief - Phase I."
