1. **Toronto and Region Conservation Authority (TRCA)**: 
   - The TRCA is the primary agency managing the project and is responsible for the overall planning, implementation, and oversight of the sanitary infrastructure protection works.
   - They conduct environmental assessments, develop strategies for erosion risk management, and lead community engagement initiatives.

2. **Local Municipalities**:
   - Local municipalities, such as the Regional Municipality of York, collaborate with TRCA to support the project's objectives.
   - They may provide funding, administrative support, and contribute local knowledge and resources necessary for the project's success.

3. **Engineering Firms (e.g., Greck and Associates Limited)**:
   - Engineering firms are contracted to provide technical expertise, conduct assessments, and propose alternative designs for infrastructure improvements.
   - They evaluate environmental impacts, offer solutions for erosion control, and oversee the implementation of engineering works in accordance with environmental standards.

4. **First Nations and Indigenous Communities**:
   - Engagement with First Nations is crucial for ensuring that Indigenous perspectives and rights are respected throughout the project.
   - Their involvement includes consultation and input into the environmental assessment process and project planning to enhance cultural and ecological considerations.

5. **Community Stakeholders**:
   - Local residents and community organizations may also play a role by participating in public consultation processes and providing feedback on project developments.
