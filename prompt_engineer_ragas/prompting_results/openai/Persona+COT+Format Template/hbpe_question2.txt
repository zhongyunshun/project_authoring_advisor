The shoreline maintenance projects outlined in the "HBPE Project Brief - Phase II" are designed with ecological considerations in mind, particularly regarding local wildlife and aquatic species. The baseline inventory section of the project documents provides insights into the project's potential impacts on aquatic habitats.

1. **Aquatic Habitat Assessment**: The project includes a detailed aquatic habitat assessment, indicating that discussions are ongoing among the Toronto and Region Conservation Authority (TRCA), the Department of Fisheries and Oceans (DFO), and other stakeholders regarding the management and development of aquatic habitats (Document 8).

2. **Invasive Species Management**: The inventory highlighted various aquatic species, including invasive species like the Round Goby (Neogobius melanostomus), which can disrupt local ecosystems by outcompeting native fish for resources (Document 10). The project's design and implementation phases consider these factors to mitigate further impacts.

3. **Sustainable Design Measures**: The project aims to employ designs that minimize habitat alteration. For instance, the proposed structures and any associated works are designed to avoid in-water activities to limit disruption to aquatic habitats (Document 9, Document 13).

4. **Compensation and Restoration Initiatives**: As part of the shoreline maintenance efforts, compensation planting and restoration measures are planned to enhance local biodiversity and restore any impacted habitats. The preliminary planting plan proposes significant on-site compensation plantings to mitigate potential adverse effects on aquatic habitats (Document 20).

In summary, the shoreline maintenance projects are structured to incorporate ecological sensitivity, particularly towards aquatic species, ensuring that comprehensive assessments and mitigation strategies are developed and implemented. These approaches aim to balance maintenance needs with the protection and enhancement of local wildlife habitats. 

For more detailed information, please refer to the "HBPE Project Brief - Phase II" documents and their supplemental materials.
