The roles and responsibilities of the Toronto and Region Conservation Authority (TRCA) and the City of Toronto in the maintenance and administration of Humber Bay Park East are outlined in the "HBPE Project Brief - Phase I." 

1. **Toronto and Region Conservation Authority (TRCA)**: 
   - **Project Management**: TRCA is responsible for managing the Humber Bay Park East Major Maintenance Project. This includes overseeing the detailed design and implementation phases of the project.
   - **Technical Expertise**: TRCA provides technical expertise, especially related to environmental assessments and shoreline protection strategies. They ensure compliance with regulatory requirements and best practices in conservation.

2. **City of Toronto**:
   - **Collaboration and Support**: The City works in collaboration with the TRCA, providing necessary support for maintenance and public access to the park. They play a role in public engagement and ensuring that community needs are met through the project.
   - **Property Management**: The City is responsible for the day-to-day administration and maintenance of the park facilities, ensuring they remain accessible and well-maintained for public use.

This collaborative approach highlights the joint effort between TRCA and the City of Toronto in maintaining and enhancing the Humber Bay Park East environment for community use and ecological sustainability.

(Source: HBPE Project Brief - Phase I, Toronto Region Conservation Authority)
