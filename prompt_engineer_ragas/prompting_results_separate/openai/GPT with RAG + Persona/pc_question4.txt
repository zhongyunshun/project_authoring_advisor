The roles and responsibilities of stakeholders involved in the Class Environmental Assessment (Class EA) for the Peacham Crescent Slope Stabilization Project, as described in the "2019-09-26 Peacham Class EA PP," include the following:

1. **Toronto and Region Conservation Authority (TRCA)**:
   - The TRCA is the lead agency responsible for managing the Class EA process. Their role includes evaluating environmental impacts, developing remediation solutions, and ensuring compliance with the Environmental Assessment Act.

2. **Public and Community Groups**:
   - Stakeholders such as homeowners in the vicinity, community groups, and Indigenous communities are involved in the consultation process. They receive notifications and are engaged through public meetings and information sessions to provide input on the project.

3. **Environmental and Engineering Consultants**:
   - Experts are often employed to conduct studies, assessments, and evaluations necessary for informed decision-making regarding the project's environmental impacts and proposed alternatives. 

4. **Ministry of the Environment**:
   - The provincial Ministry plays a role at the higher regulatory level, receiving assessments and making determinations on significant environmental impacts as they relate to the project.

5. **Local Authorities**:
   - Local government and planning authorities may provide input regarding land use, zoning, and community impacts, contributing to the decision-making process for the slope stabilization measures.

The collaborative approach ensures that various perspectives and concerns are considered throughout the Class EA process, ultimately aiming to protect human life and property while addressing environmental sustainability.
