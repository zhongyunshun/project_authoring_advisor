Based on the provided documents, here's a summary of specific engagements and consultations with local Indigenous communities detailed in the projects' planning and implementation phases:

*   **General Engagement Process:** The documents emphasize the importance of Indigenous engagement, aiming for meaningful and mutually respectful relationships with communities potentially impacted by TRCA projects (Document 4, 11, 12). Engagement is intended to provide Indigenous communities with an opportunity to receive information about and have input to the project proposal (Document 1).

    *   Hiawatha First Nation (Document 17, 19)
    *   Six Nations of the Grand River First Nation (Document 16)
    *   Williams Treaties First Nations (Document 3)
    *   Huron-Wendat Nation (Document 3, 18)
    *   Beausoleil First Nation (Document 18)
    *   Chippewas of Georgina Island First Nation (Document 18)
    *   Chippewas of Rama First Nation (Document 18)
    *   Curve Lake First Nation (Document 18)
    *   Mississaugas of Alderville First Nation (Document 18)
*   **Timing of Consultation:** Consultation occurs prior to notifications, with the Ministry of the Environment, Conservation, and Parks (MECP) and the Ministry of Indigenous Relations and Reconciliation (MIRR) being contacted for advice on which communities to engage (Document 2, 10).
*   **Methods of Communication:** Communication methods include courier and email (Document 9, 16), and providing information packages (Document 9).

*   **Record Keeping:** A full record of Indigenous community consultation, including copies of all correspondence, is maintained (Document 6).
